Relaxation

The state of rest as a result of the reduction or elimination of muscular and (or) emotional tension.

For complete relaxation and removal of excess tension, three directions of relaxation are used - muscle, respiratory or visual (mental).

Relaxation methods

Progressive muscle relaxation.

This method was developed and proposed in 1926 by the American physician, psychiatrist and physiologist Edmund Jacobson. It is based on the fact that the maximum relaxation of the muscles is preceded by their strong tension.

Autogenic training.

This self-hypnosis method was invented in 1932 by the German psychiatrist Johann Schulz. The technique is based on careful observation and memorization of bodily sensations that occur during muscle relaxation, and then on arbitrary reproduction of the sensations recorded in memory and the corresponding functional state.

Meditation.

This is a method of spiritual practice that helps to reduce physical activity as much as possible, turn off emotions and focus your attention, for example, on your breathing, bodily sensations, on a candle flame or the sound of the surf. External fuss ceases to matter. All attention is focused on the moment "here and now".

Abdominal breathing.

Helps to learn how to breathe properly, saturates the blood with oxygen. The technique is simple: inhale slowly and as deeply as possible through the nose, inflating the stomach (and not the chest), hold the breath for a few seconds, and then exhale slowly through the mouth, applying certain efforts and trying to completely empty the lungs.
